About Park Pals

Park Pals is a non-profit organization established to enhance the public's enjoyment of the Newburgh public parks along with preserving, protecting and growing the parks. Park Pals is an extension of the Newburgh Parks Board which strives to preserve, protect and manage natural, historical, and recreational resources and facilities in order to offer leisure activities and programming for all citizens.

Newburgh Public Parks

Below is a list of the public parks located within the corporate limits of Newburgh, Indiana

Indian Hill Overlook Park

This is a historic site that was once an American Indian settlement. Indian Hill Overlook Park was added to the park system in 2005 and is a popular passive recreation park along the Rivertown Trail.

Lou Dennis Community Park

This facility includes the Newburgh Community Pool, the Fortress of Fun, two tennis courts, one basketball court, one soccer field and a picnic shelter. The picnic shelter is available to rent.

Old Lock and Dam Park

Old Lock and Dam Park is adjacent to the Ohio River and is a significant site on the Rivertown Trail. The south section features a boat launch, parking and the historic Old Lock and Dam building. The north section features a gazebo, two Lockmaster Cottages, and a historic log cabin with a garden. The Old Lock and Dam and the Lockmaster Cottages are available to rent.

Reflection Park

Reflection Park is not yet constructed but will be a passive park located on a river front piece of land recently donated to the Town.

Rivertown Trail

The Rivertown Trail was constructed in two phases in 2008 & 2011, with a third phase planned to extend the trail west. The Trail extends almost the length of Newburgh, from the New Locks and Dam Park to the B. Gene Aurand Trailhead. Veterans Monument Park Veterans Monument Park is a monument plaza erected to honor all veterans, past, present and future.
